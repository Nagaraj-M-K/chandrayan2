CHANDRAYAAN2

  Chandrayaan 2 was India's second lunar exploration mission, launched by the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) with the goal of furthering our understanding of the Moon's surface and its potential resources. The mission was designed to consist of an orbiter, a lander named Vikram, and a rover named Pragyan. While the orbiter successfully entered lunar orbit and continues to operate, the lander's attempt to make a soft landing on the Moon encountered a setback.

  The landing attempt of the Vikram lander took place on September 6, 2019, and it was closely watched by people around the world. Unfortunately, during the final stages of the descent, communication with the lander was lost, and it crashed on the lunar surface. The failure of the landing was a disappointment, but it's important to recognize that space exploration is challenging and risky, and failures are a part of the learning process.

Despite the landing setback, Chandrayaan 2 achieved several important objectives:

  • ORBITER SUCSES:

    •   The Chandrayaan 2 orbiter continued to function and is still operational as of my last knowledge update in September 2021. The orbiter has been sending back valuable data and images of the Moon's surface, contributing to our understanding of lunar geology and mineralogy.

  • TECHNOLOGICAL ADVANCEMENT :

    •   The mission demonstrated India's technological capabilities in terms of launching and operating a mission to the Moon, as well as the complex maneuvers involved in lunar orbit insertion.

  • INSPIRATION AND LEARNING:

    •   Chandrayaan 2 captured the attention and interest of the global community, inspiring people, especially young minds, to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

  • International Collaboration:

    •   Chandrayaan 2 involved international collaboration by carrying scientific instruments from other countries, showcasing India's willingness to cooperate and contribute to global space research.
